What meetings are in each category? The IAAF determines to which Category a given competition should be assigned. There are 4 high ranked categories followed by 6 more categories. • OW - Olympic Games, World Outdoor Championships • DF - Diamond League Finals • GW - World Indoor Championships, World Cross Country / half-marathon / Race Walking Championships, Continental Cup, Diamond League Meetings • GL - Senior Area Championships, Hammer Throw / Combined / Race Walking Challenge, Gold Label Marathons

There are 6 more categories, named from A'-F', in which

• Meeting category B' are considered to be the Senior National Outdoor Championships.

Note: Foreign athletes do not receive any points from place

How many competitions do I need in order to be in IAAF's ranking? You will need to have at least 5 competitions in total, with 3 of them being on the main event (which means that you can have up to 2 similar events out of 5 that will be counted) with a few exceptions: • For the 3000m steeple and the 5000m, you will need 3 competitions in total, with at least 2 of them being on the main event. • For the 10.000m., you will need 2 competitions in total, with at least 1 of them being on the main event. • For the combined events, you will need 2 competitions in total, with at least 1 of them being on the main event. • For the road running group (10km and half-marathon), as well as for the 20km race walking, you will need 3 competitions in total, with at least 2 of them being on the main event. • For the Marathon, as well as for the 50km race walking, you will need 2 competitions in total, with at least 1 of them being on the main event.

For how long are my competition's points in effect? • Ranking points are considered to be in effect for a 12-month period. Note that after the 9th month, there is a deduction of 20 points per month and for every month until the full 12-month period is passed • For the combined events, 10.000m and all road running events, ranking points are considered to be in effect for a 18-month period, without any deduction. • The points that have been earned from OW & DF meeting categories, are considered to be in effect up until the next meeting of the same category. •The points that have been earned from an outdoor's area championship, are considered to be in effect up until the next one, despite the fact that the 12-month (or 18-month) period might have been completed.
